Testing an Online Support for Teens with Recent Self-Harm

Study Rationale: Many teenagers do not access formal or consistent mental health treatment for self-harm, and a majority seek out information or help for their mental health online. I evaluated whether one online resource, “Project SAVE”, could help teenage users (1) reduce their self-harm behaviors, and (2) feel better about themselves. I designed and conducted an A/B test, administering surveys to understand changes in self-hatred and self-harm over time.

Research Question: Can this online support tool help teenagers reduce how frequently they harm themselves? Can it help reduce self-hate?

Findings & Recommendations: The Project SAVE support tool did not reduce teenagers’ self-harm behavior three months later. Right after Project SAVE, teenagers reported reductions in self-hatred. There was clear demand for anonymous mental health resources for self-harm among teens, backed by qualitative feedback and high completion rates. I recommended integrating a streamlined Project SAVE in online communities of teenagers experiencing self-harm and self-hate, who likely do not have timely access to other supports.

Method: A/B testing

565 teenagers with a recent history of self-harm were randomly assigned to one of two, time-matched versions of an online support tool: one providing standard supportive care, and one teaching specific strategies to manage self-harm urges (Project SAVE). Questionnaires assessed self-hatred and desire to stop self-harm behavior immediately before and after each of the two tools. Teenagers provided written qualitative (“What would you change about the program?”) and quantitative feedback (Likert scales) on both tools. 3 months later, teenagers from both groups received questionnaires about self-harm. I used pre-registered intent-to-treat analyses with multiple imputation to minimize the impact of missing data on results.

Full details available in our published paper, analysis code, podcast, and infographic.

Insight #1: Decreased self-hatred

SAVE decreased teenagers’ self-hatred, relative to those assigned to standard supportive care:

Intent-to-treat regression analyses detected significant differences between SAVE and standard supportive care groups (p < .001, d = -.35), controlling for teenagers’ pre-test values. Results remained consistent across multiple sensitivity analyses.

Recommendation: Integrate SAVE into online settings where quick, short-term improvements in self-hate are especially valuable (e.g., intercepting users seeking information about self-hate or self-harm online).

Insight #2: No decrease in self-harm

SAVE did not reduce self-harm 3 months later, relative to those assigned to standard supportive care:

Intent-to-treat regression analyses found no significant differences between SAVE and standard supportive care groups (p =.67, d = -.04), controlling for teenagers’ pre-test values. Results remained consistent across multiple sensitivity analyses.

Recommendation: Integrate SAVE into online settings where quick, short-term improvements in self-hate are especially valuable (e.g., intercepting users seeking information about self-hate or self-harm online).

Insight #3: High completion rate

A majority of teenagers who started SAVE (80%) finished the entire program.

Recommendation: Evaluate completion rates in settings outside of randomized trials to better estimate real-world applications.

Insight #4: Demand for anonymous supports

Teenagers liked that the support was anonymous (see qualitative survey feedback):

User 1: “I really like how it feels safe and anonymous. It makes me feel comfortable to answer honestly.”
User 2: “The questions gave me a way to express things I’m too afraid to tell other people. It made me a little happy knowing someone cares.”

Recommendation: Continue to develop, evaluate, and disseminate anonymous mental health support options to teens who may be dealing with self-harm.

Insight #5: Demand for shorter support

Teenagers wished the tool was shorter (see qualitative survey feedback):

User 3: “It was kinda long. For kids with adhd like myself, its hard to focus... The illustrations really helped bring my focus together and make sure i don't zone out.”

Recommendation: Streamline SAVE to remove redundant content and prevent churn.
