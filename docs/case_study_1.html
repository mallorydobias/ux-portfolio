Evaluating A Mental Health Peer Support Tool For Discord

Study Rationale: Analyses of user onboarding patterns showed high churn rates between setup and activation of a Discord-based mental health peer support tool. I conducted user interviews and usability testing with Discord server moderators (“mods”) to understand reasons for user churn, identify common pain points, and develop recommendations for future product iterations.

Research Question: Why are mods not completing the onboarding process to install this peer support service in their servers?

Findings & Recommendations: Mods were not understanding the product’s value proposition or functionality as they progressed through onboarding. This included confusion about whether the tool’s ‘peer-support’ involves connecting with other people, as well as uncertainty about how their posts would be shared (and with whom). I recommended editing the tool’s webpage and streamlining onboarding copy to facilitate a smoother, clearer onboarding flow.

Method: User interviews, moderated usability testing

8 Discord users with moderation experience were recruited from Prolific for user interviews. Post-interview, each participant completed usability testing for the tool’s promotional webpage, installation, and setup.

Insight #1: Unclear landing page

Based on the product's landing page, mods were uncertain whether ‘peer-support’ involved connecting with other people, a chatbot, or resource provision (see interview quotes):

User 1: “I’m surprised. I thought it would give you resources. I didn’t think it would connect you with other Discord users."
User 2: “I'm not sure if it would have an AI that would interact with you, or if it also would be real people – this suggests it could be either.”

Recommendation: Edit landing page to emphasize users will be connected with real people.

Insight #2: Lengthy onboarding

Even for mods who anticipated being connected with other people, they began to doubt this initial impression as the onboarding process continues (see interview quote):

User 4: “At this point, I’m not sure if it is only scripted, I mean, automated AI… is it going to connect me to someone?"

Recommendation 1: Streamline onboarding copy to more quickly connect users with peers.

Recommendation 2: Add very brief updates (e.g., “X more steps until you’re connected with other Koko users just like you!”) to reassure users during onboarding.

Insight #3: Unclear posting functionality

Mods were surprised that anonymous posts are shared to a public vent channel in their server (see interview quote):

User 3: “Oh woah, I didn’t expect for my post to go the channel. I thought it was just the DMs [direct messages]”

Recommendation: Before posting, ask users if they would like to share anonymously on their server's vent channel, or only through direct message to their matched peer.

Insight #4: Lots of anticipated questions

Mods anticipated having to answer lots of questions about the tool in their servers. Some doubted whether people will use the tool altogether (see interview quote):

User 6: “If they’re unfamiliar with it, they probably want to know more… they may not even realize or trust that it’s anonymous. So, more information may make it more likely they’ll use it.”

Recommendation: Automatically add a pinned comment where users first engage with the tool (i.e., the public vent channel) to emphasize anonymity and link to FAQs.
